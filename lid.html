Automatic Language Identification (LiD)

All about LiD - Its origins and my attempts

What is it?

LiD is the black, black art of using computers to automatically make a decision on what language they are 'hearing'. I say hearing, but more often than not it's the analysis of a large corpora of spoken language data.

Put more academically;

Automatic language identification is the problem of identifying the language being spoken from a sample of speech by an unknown speaker. (Muthusamy 1993)

And even more academically;

...the language of a given utterance can be identified automatically - that is, by analytic procedures without the intervention of a human listener... (House &amp Neuburg 1977)

Put less academically;

DERR HuRRR nnerrrrrr (Me, just now)

Us mere humans can make a reasonable estimate on what language we are listening to within a number of seconds, using some rather clever stuff in our heads which nobody quite fully understands yet.

Computational Linguists, Computer Scientists and even mere university undergraduates have tackled the problem, with a variety of backgrounds and approaches.

At the last count, there were 6,909 distinct natural languages and dialects spoken on planet Earth, but only 6% of these are spoken by 94% of the global population. Given this rather large gap, LiD presents itself as a practical solution to many natural language driven problems.

LiD has been investigated as early as 1974 and since then the number of researchers interested in the field has been steadily increasing. Below you can find links to my favourite papers and resources on Google Scholar.
